The Holocaust

The Holocaust was the systematic, bureaucratic, state-sponsored persecution and murder of six million Jewish people by the Nazi regime and its collaborators. It was a watershed moment in history that calls for constant remembrance [4].

Key Historical Context

  • 1933-1939: Rise of Nazism and isolation of Jews.
  • 1939-1942: Ghettoization and mobile killing units.
  • 1942-1945: The "Final Solution" - Extermination camps [4].

Testimony & Memory

“The opposite of love is not hate, it's indifference.” — Elie Wiesel. Preserving the stories of survivors ensures that the lessons of this atrocity are not lost to future generations.
